How Solar Panels Work

Solar panels use photovoltaic (PV) cells, typically made of silicon, to convert sunlight into electricity. When sunlight hits the panels, photons excite electrons in the semiconductor material, creating an electric current via the photovoltaic effect. This direct current (DC) is converted to alternating current (AC) by an inverter for home use.

The process involves: 1) Sunlight absorption, 2) Electron excitation, 3) Current generation, and 4) Inversion to usable power. Solar systems can include batteries for storage.
